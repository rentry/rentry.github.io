06.03.05

i was reading walker percy today...the philosophy, not the literature. i was struck by his theory of rotation, a category he pulls from kierkegaard but modifies. i was imagining this place in its pristine and seemingly changeless beauty as one that is exempt from the process of rotation. rotation implies a transformation, an encounter with mystery that shakes and alters the foundation of the mind. but, as percy elucidates the theory, the encounter is typically, if not necessarily, a confrontation with the radically other, the un-encountered, the uncharted.

in this place, every day is different. the landscape is transformed entirely in the course of a night. it is the same the next day, to a certain extent, and has been for the last 500 years or so. and still, the alteration of the spiritual landscape is almost impossible to describe. it makes me hope that all of the people from oregon who said they were going to come visit actually do.
