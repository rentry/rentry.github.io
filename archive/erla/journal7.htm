06.15.05

yesterday, on the way to powell, we observed the weighing and tagging of hummingbirds at the home of paul and helen nees. it was amazing. they contain the birds, place them in silk sleeves, and document statistics pertaining primarily to migratory habits. we found out that 15% of the birds return to a given sight after spending their winter in mexico. incredible birds.

powell, wyoming, my home town, is a booming metropolis at 5373 persons. when i left, the official census data was 5292. now if that isn't out of control growth (ha!), i don't know what is. when heraclitus said that no one could step in the same river twice, he was also referring to home towns. we also visited the buffalo bill historic center in cody, which is much more interesting after one has moved away for six years.

my grandma zona is a phenomenal woman, by the way, and if i received one percent of her genes, it was more than i deserved. --rj
