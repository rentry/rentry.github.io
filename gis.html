Geographic Information Science

I completed my Geographic Information Science (GIScience) graduate certificate in 2013 from Oregon State University. Those familiar with GIS will recognize this slight alteration from the more conventional “Geographic Information Systems.” The OSU program inspires students to think beyond tools and analyze processes and approach.

In the graduate program, I focused on watershed health. The poster below details an analysis I conducted to prioritize riparian restoration.

Following the graduate program, I had the opportunity to work with the Oregon Department of Fish and Wildlife and the Institute for Natural Resources to support the accuracy, consistency and standardization of metadata for the Nearshore Ecological Data Atlas. I also supported the development and distribution of training materials for the Oregon Coastal Management Program.
